    About

    Here are a few things you might like to know about the Indie Recommender tool, and Indie games in general.

    Game recommendations are based on data from two sources.

    The first source is Metacritic PC game listings. From Metacritic, this app uses summary descriptions of games, their titles, and genre information (like "Sci-Fi") to classify the game.

    Similar information is obtained about Indie games from IndieDB.com.

    The genres are generally used to "slice" through the data. For instance, if a Metacritic game is a shooter game, the app will search for Indie shooter games.

    The similarity score is given in a range from 0 to 100. If a game has a similarity score of 100, that means the summary text of the Indie game and the Metacritic game is exactly the same. If it's 0, then the text is completely different.

    Most good matches have values of above 20. Matches at 15-20 may be decent, but may be suspect. Very high match scores (above 30, and often far above) may indicate that the games are the same game, or closely related. Sequels, perhaps.

    While most of these "good matches" will be fine, there are some failures. These are mostly in cases where a game is miscategorized, or poorly summarized, in at least one of the two databases.

    The Indie game "Thomas Was Alone" is an example of this. In the Metacritic database, it's referred to as an adventure game narrated by someone from Assassin's Creed. Conversely, in the IndieDB, it is described as a pleasant platformer about friendship.

    Finally, the "User Rating" listed on the front page is the average user rating of the game on the IndieDB website. If the rating value is -1, then no one has rated the game yet.

    Possible future features include:

  • Incorporating reviews into the similarity scoring process.
  • Inclusion of Indie games' Metacritic scores where they are available.
